# How It Looks (and Why)

Burrow's strongest design move is having almost no design. In a world of hyper-designed startups with custom illustration systems, mascots, and brand guidelines longer than novels — Burrow's visual identity is defined by absence.

---

Typography.

Two fonts. Forever.

JetBrains Mono — for everything structural. Navigation, addresses, file names, metadata, directory listings. This is the surface of Burrow. It says: this is a tool, not a magazine.

Literata — for reading. When you open a text file, the content renders in Literata. Warm, highly legible serif. It says: someone wrote this, and it deserves your attention.

No other fonts. No font-size buffet. Three sizes: 14px for UI, 17px for reading, 13px for metadata. That's the entire type scale.

---

Color.

The entire UI is built from five values:

  surface:    almost-white background
  text:       almost-black text
  muted:      secondary text, borders
  faint:      subtle backgrounds
  accent:     teal — links, one interactive element

Dark mode inverts surface and text. The accent stays. That's the whole theme engine.

Why no brand color? Because brand colors are marketing. They exist to make you feel something about a company. Burrow isn't trying to make you feel anything about Burrow. It's trying to get out of the way so you feel something about the content.

Burrow owners can set a custom accent color in their .burrow config. Your burrow, your tint. Everything else stays neutral.

---

Icons.

Burrow uses zero icons. Every affordance is communicated through:

Text labels — Publish, Search, Settings
Punctuation as symbols — / for directory, → for link, ? for search, ★ for bookmark
Spatial relationships — indentation is hierarchy, position is priority

Why? Because icons are ambiguous. What does a hamburger menu mean? What does a gear icon do? What's the difference between a share icon and an export icon? Text is never ambiguous. Text is Gopher's first principle.

---

The reading experience.

When you open a text file, the UI disappears. No sidebar. No toolbar. Just text, rendered in Literata at 17px, with comfortable 1.7 line-height and a max width of 65 characters.

The background is warm off-white, not harsh white. The text is soft almost-black, not pure black. The effect is subtle but visceral: it feels like paper, not a screen.

A thin 2px line at the top fills as you scroll. When you reach the end, it pulses once and fades. That's the only UI element visible while reading.

Below the title, a ghost line appears for three seconds: ~4 min read. Then it fades. You know what you're committing to, but the information doesn't linger and compete with the words.

---

The anti-brand.

No mascot. No illustrations. No stock photography. No hero images. No logo animation. No brand video. No swag. (Okay, maybe one t-shirt: plain black, white monospace text: gph://~you.)

If you use Burrow for a week and someone asks what it looks like, the answer should be: "I don't remember what it looks like. I remember what I read."
