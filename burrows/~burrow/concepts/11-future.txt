# Where This Is Going

If Burrow succeeds, five years from now:

Half a million people have burrows across hundreds of servers. Writers, researchers, and thinkers use it as their primary publishing platform. Veronica-NG is the first place people search when they want thoughtful writing instead of SEO-optimized AI slop.

The Burrow protocol is documented, stable, and has multiple independent implementations. The HTTPS gateway means Burrow content ranks in Google — and ranks well, because it loads fast, it's accessible, and it's human-written.

The company is profitable, small, and has never taken venture capital.

---

What's already shipped (and what's next).

Custom domains: done. Add aliases to burrow.conf and your burrow answers to multiple domain names. The server reads the Host header and adjusts breadcrumbs, feed URLs, and canonical references. DNS is your problem.

Full-text search: done. BM25 ranking, freshness boost, search operators (author:, type:, fresh:). The index builds at startup. Veronica-NG lives at /search.

Federation: done. Outgoing pings on startup, incoming POST /ping endpoint, mentions displayed on posts. The simplest possible distributed system.

What's coming next.

The Tauri desktop client. A native window that speaks gph://, renders in Literata, and makes the keyboard feel like a superpower. Under 8 MB. Starts in under a second.

The mobile reader. iOS and Android. Reading, bookmarking, discovering. No publishing — that's deliberate.

Reading challenges. Community goals tracked locally. No leaderboards, no gamification. Just a personal counter and the satisfaction of reaching a goal you set for yourself.

The landing page. burrow.sh — the manifesto, the waitlist, the first impression. Just text. Fast. Beautiful. The product as its own advertisement.

---

The biggest risk isn't technical. It's cultural.

Burrow has to be cool enough for early adopters but useful enough for everyone else. Punk but not pretentious. Minimal but not austere. Nostalgic but not backwards.

The answer is in the name. A burrow is small, warm, and yours. That's always been the promise of the personal internet. We just forgot, for a while, what it felt like.
