# Why Plaintext Matters — Part 1: The Problem

The average web page in 2026 weighs 2.8 MB. Most of that weight is not your words.

It's trackers. Ad networks. Analytics scripts. A consent banner more complex than the content you came for. Font files for fonts you didn't ask for. JavaScript bundles that execute before you've read a single sentence.

Your words — the actual reason the page exists — account for maybe 5 KB of that 2.8 MB. That's 0.2%. The rest is infrastructure for monetizing your attention.
