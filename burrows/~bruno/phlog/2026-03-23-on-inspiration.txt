# On Inspiration

Maya wrote something that stuck with me. About how removing things from your digital life isn't loss — it's clarity. About how the empty space left behind isn't empty at all.

I've been thinking about that ever since.

We build tools that demand attention. Then we build more tools to manage the attention the first tools demanded. It's tools all the way down. At some point someone has to ask: what if we just... stopped?

Not stopped building. Stopped demanding.

What if the tool said: "Here are your words. Here is a quiet place to read them. I have nothing else to say."

That's what we're trying to do here. Not because minimalism is trendy. Because noise is the default, and silence takes effort.

Thanks, Maya.
