# Finding Things Worth Reading

The modern internet has a discovery problem. Algorithms show you what keeps you scrolling, not what's worth reading. Search engines optimize for SEO, not quality. Social media amplifies outrage, not insight.

Burrow has four discovery mechanisms. None of them involve an algorithm.

---

Rings.

A webring is a curated loop of burrows — a navigable circle of related voices. Someone you trust says "these writers belong together" and links them in a ring. When you're reading a post from a ring member, arrows appear at the bottom:

  ← Previous · Deep Web Craft · Next →

Click through and you're in someone else's burrow. It's channel surfing for thoughtful writing. The ring was created by a human, not a model. The curation is personal, opinionated, imperfect. That's why it works.

Rings span servers. A ring can link /~bruno on your local server and gph://tilde.town/~river on another. Rings are how Burrow federates socially — not through a protocol spec, but through human curation.

---

Bookmarks.

Burrow has one social signal: public bookmarks. When you bookmark a page, it appears at /~you/bookmarks. Your bookmarks are your taste, on display.

Bookmark counts are aggregated on the Discover page as "Most bookmarked." This is the only ranking signal in Burrow. No likes. No views. Just: how many people thought this was worth saving.

Importantly: the author never sees a number on their page. There's no "142 bookmarks!" badge. The count only affects search ranking and the Discover page. This prevents the dopamine loop of writing-for-likes. You write because you have something to say, not because you're chasing a number.

---

Search.

Veronica-NG is a full-text search engine built into burrowd. Named after Gopher's original search engine (Very Easy Rodent-Oriented Net-wide Index to Computerized Archives — the '90s were wild).

No external service. No Elasticsearch cluster. No 4 GB Java heap. Just an in-memory inverted index built from your filesystem on startup. Results ranked by BM25 with a freshness boost. Same query, same results, for everyone. No personalization. No filter bubble.

Servers can export their index as JSON at /search/index.json. Other servers import it for cross-server search. Federation by URL. The simplest model that could possibly work.

---

The firehose.

Every post, every burrow, reverse chronological order. Unranked. Unfiltered. Intentionally overwhelming. It's not meant to be read cover-to-cover. It's meant to be glanced at — to sense the weather. "A lot of people are writing about Rust today." "There's a cluster of climate posts."

The firehose is the anti-algorithm. And the random burrow link on the Discover page is StumbleUpon for plaintext — a different hole on every page load.
