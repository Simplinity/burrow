# Gemini Protocol

burrowd speaks Gemini. Because when the Gemini community built a protocol even more minimal than Gopher, we felt seen.

---

Add three lines to burrow.conf:

  gemini_port = 1965
  tls_cert = /path/to/cert.pem
  tls_key = /path/to/key.pem

Restart burrowd. It now listens on port 1965 alongside HTTP. Same content, different protocol. Your .gph files are auto-converted to Gemtext on the fly.

The conversion is nearly 1:1 because .gph was designed with Gemini in mind:

  # Heading       stays   # Heading
  > Quote         stays   > Quote
  → URL           becomes => URL
  /~user   Desc   becomes => /~user Desc
  code blocks     get triple backticks
  ---             becomes a blank line
  plain text      stays plain text

Directory listings render as Gemini link pages. The home page lists all burrows. Every route that works over HTTP works over Gemini.

---

Why Gemini matters: the Gemini community is small but passionate. They care about the same things we care about — simplicity, privacy, user agency, beautiful plaintext. Burrow should embrace them, not compete with them.

The Gemini bridge is a statement: we're building a protocol, not a walled garden. We talk to other protocols. Your words are not trapped here.

---

Test with any Gemini client:

  amfora gemini://localhost:1965/~bruno/about

Or the hard way:

  echo "gemini://localhost/~bruno/about" | openssl s_client -connect localhost:1965

Both work. Both show your about.txt rendered as Gemtext. Neither required you to change a single character of your content.
