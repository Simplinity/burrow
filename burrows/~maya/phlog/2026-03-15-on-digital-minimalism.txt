# On digital minimalism

People keep asking me what apps I use. They expect a list. They want the productivity stack, the note-taking workflow, the task management system.

My answer disappoints them: I use a text editor and a file system.

Every app is a set of opinions about how you should work, wrapped in a subscription fee. Every app wants to be the center of your digital life. Every app sends notifications.

I don't want opinions. I want a blank page.

---

Burrow is the first platform I've used in years that doesn't try to tell me what to do. It gives me a directory and says: put things in it.

No templates. No getting started wizard. No AI writing assistant. Just an empty folder with my name on it.

The emptiness is not a bug. It is the feature.
