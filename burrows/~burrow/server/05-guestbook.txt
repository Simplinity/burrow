# Guestbook

It's not the '90s internet without one.

---

  burrow guestbook init

This creates a guestbook.gph file in your burrow. Visitors can sign it at /~yourname/guestbook. There's a form. It's plain HTML. No JavaScript. It works in Lynx. It works in curl if you're that kind of person. It works in the browser your grandma uses.

Entries look like this:

  --- Nostalgic Gopher · 2026-03-22 01:56
  Your site loads faster than my thoughts. Respect.

  --- Anonymous · 2026-03-22 14:30
  I haven't seen a guestbook since GeoCities. I'm not crying.

  --- xXx_DarkCoder_xXx · 2026-03-22 15:12
  tried to XSS your guestbook. it escaped my script tag.
  i respect that. signing the book instead.

---

Limits, because the internet is why we can't have nice things:

Name: 40 characters. You're signing a book, not writing your LinkedIn headline.

Message: 500 characters. A note, not a blog post. You have your own burrow for that.

Total entries: 200 per guestbook. After 200, start a new one. Or bask in your popularity.

Rate: one signature per 30 seconds per IP. Spammers will need to be very patient.

Format injection: if you type --- in your message, it becomes an em dash. Nice try with the entry separators.

---

Read entries from the terminal:

  burrow guestbook show

They print with name, date, and message. It's like checking the visitor book at a museum, except the museum is your personal corner of the internet and the visitors are strangers who care about plaintext.
